Load (kg/yr) = concentration (mg/L) × flow (m³/day) × days ÷ 1000 (US EPA DMR method). Each facility is tagged to the loaded district/AEZ so totals roll up by area. ▲ flags loads over the entered permit limit. Click a header to sort.
DATA · Imagery: Sentinel-2 / Harmonized Landsat–Sentinel (30 m) and true/false-colour composites from NASA GIBS — physical proxies for turbidity, suspended sediment and algal/eutrophication blooms; satellites cannot measure chromium, BOD, ammonia or other chemicals, and clear-looking water can still be polluted. River flow (dilution context): GloFAS via the free Open-Meteo Flood API. Geography: 64 districts and the 30 BARC agro-ecological zones embedded in this file (district-centroid coordinates; many districts span several AEZs). Loading register: US EPA Water Pollutant Loading Tool / DMR method (load = concentration × flow × time), with relative toxic-weighting for ranking. It organizes and ranks reported/sampled effluent data — it does not measure or invent concentrations. In Bangladesh the authoritative effluent and ambient water-quality data and standards rest with the Department of Environment (ECR) and BWDB; this is a screening and prioritization tool, not a compliance determination. No API keys; runs entirely in the browser.
